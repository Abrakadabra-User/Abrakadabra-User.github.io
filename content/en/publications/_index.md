## Peer-reviewed Publications

*   **Fu, Z.**, et al. (2026). **"SARM: Scene-Aware Retinex Mamba for Underwater Image Enhancement."** *Remote Sensing*, 18(10), 1652.
    *   **Key Contribution**: Proposed SARM, a self-supervised framework integrating Retinex theoretical priors with State Space Models (SSM). By leveraging a Scene-Aware Adapter (SAA) and a 2D selective scan mechanism with linear computational complexity O(HW), the framework effectively suppresses non-uniform scattering noise and color casts in underwater scenarios while achieving state-of-the-art performance.

## Competitions Reports

*   **2026 Mathematical Contest in Modeling (MCM/ICM)**
    *   **Award**: **Finalist (Top 1% Worldwide)**
    *   **Project**: "The Invisible Spotlight: Reconstructing Fan Votes and Optimizing Scoring Mechanisms"
    *   **Key Contribution**: Addressed the opacity of reality television voting systems by developing an Inverse Bayesian Reconstruction Model (IBRM) and a Counterfactual Simulation Engine. Combined Ant Colony Optimization (ACO) with Linear Mixed Models (LMM) to establish a hybrid evaluation framework, culminating in an F1-inspired dynamic scoring system that achieves Pareto optimality between commercial entertainment and professional competitive integrity.

*   **2025 Mathematical Contest in Modeling (MCM/ICM)**
    *   **Award**: **Meritorious Winner (H Award)**
    *   **Project**: "Can Olympic Medals Be Predicted?"
    *   **Key Contribution**: Constructed a multi-dimensional mathematical framework utilizing linear, logistic, and negative binomial regression models to quantify the impact of national economic strength, population size, and the "Great Coach" effect on Olympic medal distribution. Employed ARIMA time-series analysis for robust forecasting and conducted sensitivity analysis to validate the model's reliability across varying scenarios.
